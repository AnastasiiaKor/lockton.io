This is a platform that enables the implementation and deployment of financial organizations in a regulated environment, supported by trusted institutional participants (government authorities).

lockton.one

Whitepaper

At a foundational level, the platform:

  • Organizes role and permission management;
  • Offers a user and company adaptation flow in response to the necessary regulatory framework;
  • Establishes policies that control limits and potential asset flows between all involved parties;
  • Provides asset management mechanisms, including direct issuance on the platform and integration with other infrastructures;
  • Enables the development of custom applications using smart contracts and the Ethereum virtual machine. This allows developers to reuse a vast existing code base of products and tools;
  • Offers tools for integrating traditional and web3 systems - payment services, financial organizations, depositories, loyalty programs, marketplaces, metaverses, etc.;
  • Features built-in activity audit for applications with the ability to offload part of their logic to L2, achieving confidentiality and provenance (compliance with rules embedded on L1).

The first licensed crypto depository in Uzbekistan. Lockton Finance is a platform for asset issuance, primary placement, and storage, licensed by the National Agency of Perspective Projects of the Republic of Uzbekistan. The platform is equipped with an on-chain KYC module with the possibility of connecting to a third-party KYC provider in the future. Each operation on the platform is recorded in its own blockchain.

lockton.uz

Lockton Finance platform can provide the following services:

  • Deposit programs
  • Possibility of fiat placements (UZS)
  • The internal token LCH is pegged to the value of US Treasury bonds and is therefore not subject to devaluation relative to the US dollar.
  • ITO as a service. ITO is the procedure of token issuance, registration, initial offering, and placement.
  • Tokenization of assets
  • Storage and placement of issued assets
  • Accounting for other types of collateral
